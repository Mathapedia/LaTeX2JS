Basic Mathematics

When $a \ne 0$, there are two solutions to $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ and they are:

$$x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}$$

The Lorenz Equations

\begin{align} \dot{x} &= \sigma(y-x) \\ \dot{y} &= \rho x - y - xz \\ \dot{z} &= -\beta z + xy \end{align}

Famous Integrals

Some inline math: $E = mc^2$ and $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}$

$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} dx = \sqrt{\pi}$$

Maxwell's Equations

\begin{align} \nabla \times \mathbf{E} &= -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t}\\ \nabla \times \mathbf{B} &= \mu_0\mathbf{J} + \mu_0\epsilon_0\frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t}\\ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} &= \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}\\ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} &= 0 \end{align}

Series and Summations
